Elway's Biography
  • Born June 28. 1960 in Port Angeles, Washington
  • Attended Stanford University
  • Played Quarterback for the Broncos for 16 years (1983 - 1999)
  • Drafted in the first round, first pick in 1983
  • Wore Jersey #7 for Denver
John Elway’s NFL Records
  • Most wins by a starting Quarterback
  • Second highest passing percentage in NFL History
  • Started in 5 different Super Bowls
  • One of two players in NFL history to score a rushing touchdown in 4 different Super Bowls. The other player was Thurman Thomas.
  • Started in 5 different Super Bowls. This was a record until Super Bowl XLIX.
John Elways’s Career Achievements
  • Won Super Bowl XXXII vs. Green Bay Packers 31-24
  • Won Super Bowl XXXIII vs. Atlanta Falcons 34-19
  • Voted MVP of Super Bowl XXXIII, which was the last game Elway played in as he retired after the 1998-1999 season was over.
  • Led the Broncos to a notable 6 AFC Championship Games
  • Led the Broncos to 5 Super Bowls. They won back to back Super Bowls in 1998 and 1999.
John Elway’s Post-NFL Career

John Elway is now the general manager and executive vice president of football operations for the Denver Broncos.
Since Elway took the job of the general manager of the Broncos, the team has the following accomplishments:

  • Won 4 Division Titles
  • Won 2 AFC Championships
  • Won Super Bowl 50
  • Inducted into the NFL Hall of Fame in 2004
  • Inducted into the College Football Hall of Fame in 2004
  • Enterpreneured a business called “Elways Motors”; a car dealership in Denver.
